## 3. Regulatory Landscape

### 3.1 EU AI Act

The EU AI Act is the most comprehensive AI regulation globally. Its phased enforcement creates escalating compliance requirements through 2027.

**Key Requirements for High-Risk AI:**

| Requirement | Article | Enforcement Date |
|---|---|---|
| Risk management system | Art. 9 | August 2026 |
| Data governance | Art. 10 | August 2026 |
| Technical documentation | Art. 11 | August 2026 |
| Record-keeping (logging) | Art. 12 | August 2026 |
| Transparency | Art. 13 | August 2026 |
| Human oversight | Art. 14 | August 2026 |
| Accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity | Art. 15 | August 2026 |

**Penalties:** Up to EUR 35M or 7% of global annual turnover (whichever is higher) for the most serious violations.

**ACF Relevance:** ACF provides structural compliance with Articles 9, 11, 12, 13, and 14 through its constitutional substrate, protocol documentation, governance audit trail, and Human Imperative Metric. ### 3.2 United States

The US regulatory landscape is fragmented across executive orders, agency guidance, and state-level legislation:

- **Executive Order 14110** (October 2023) — Reporting requirements, safety standards, red-teaming mandates
- **NIST AI Risk Management Framework** — Voluntary governance framework
- **State-level legislation** — California, Colorado, Illinois, and others have enacted or proposed AI-specific laws
- **SEC and FTC actions** — Enforcement actions around AI claims and consumer protection

**Trend:** Movement toward mandatory governance requirements, particularly for high-risk applications.
